Ion Formation & Electron Exchange

Atoms always crave a full outer shell to be completely relaxed and stable (usually 8 valence electrons). To achieve this, some metallic atoms **donate** electrons, while non-metals gladly **receive** them! This process creates electrical charges.

Chlorine (Cl) Atom (Neutral)

💡 Metals like Sodium lose negative electrons, turning them into positively-charged ions (cations)! Non-metals like Chlorine gain them, turning them negative (anions).

Ionisation Effect of Nuclear Radiation

When unstable radioactive atoms decay, they shoot out high-energy particles (Alpha, Beta, or Gamma rays). As this radiation crashes into surrounding matter, it strips away orbital electrons. This hazardous process is called **Ionisation**!

Alpha Particles (α): Very heavy. Massive collision impact, producing highly dense ionisation along a short path.
Beta Particles (β): Light electrons. Moderate speed, medium ionisation power, penetrates slightly deeper.
Gamma Rays (γ): Pure high-frequency electromagnetic energy. Low direct ionisation rates but incredibly deep penetration.
