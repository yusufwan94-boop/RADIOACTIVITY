Decay Half-Life

Half-life (T½) is the duration required for half of a radioactive sample's nuclei to decay into a stable isotope.

50 Bq! (21 hours / 7 hours = 3 half lives. 400 → 200 → 100 → 50)

Nuclear Chain Reactor

During nuclear fission, a single neutron strikes a Uranium-235 nucleus, splitting it and releasing energy plus **3 new fast neutrons**. If these neutrons strike neighboring atoms, an explosive **chain reaction** cascades.

0% (Runaway Danger) 100% (Subcritical Shielded)

How does a nuclear reactor safely control a runaway chain reaction?

By inserting boron control rods which absorb excess neutrons, stopping the cascade!

Why is Beta radiation chosen for sheet metal thickness control instead of Alpha or Gamma?

Alpha cannot penetrate metal sheets at all, while Gamma would penetrate completely without any change in reading!
